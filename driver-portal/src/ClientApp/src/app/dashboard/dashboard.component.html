Understanding Case Statuses


Opened – This is the date your case was opened at RoadSafetyBC.

Open Pending Submission – This case has been opened by requesting a document (as opposed to receiving a submission), and has not yet had any documents submitted to it.

Under Review – The case has at least one document submitted to it, and has not yet rendered a decision.

Decision Rendered – A decision has been rendered, and a (usually) 30 day timer is underway for follow-ups, challenges, or additional documents that might change things.

Resolved – The case is now closed. If new information is received, a new case will be opened rather than adding to this case.

Definitions

Case Type
Find below the various case types.
  • Driver's Medical Examination Report – Cases opened because a Driver's Medical Examination Report was issued.
  • Reported Medical Condition - Cases opened due to receiving a high-priority medical report.
  • Police Report – Cases opened due to receiving a police report related to medical fitness to drive.
  • Unsolicited Report - Cases opened due to receiving a report from a member of the public raising road safety concerns.
  • Other - Cases opened due to receiving other documents not noted above
  • RSBC - Cases manually created/opened by RoadSafetyBC staff
  • Legacy - Legacy cases migrated from the old legacy system

DMER Type Decision outcome

  • Fit to Drive – Driver is fit to driveext to come

Eligible License Class
License classes include:
  • None – Information to come
  • Class 1 - Any motor vehicle or combination of motor vehicles, except motorcycles
  • Class 2 – All class 5 vehicles plus all public passenger-carrying vehicles
  • Class 3 – All class 5 vehicles plus any motor vehicle with 3 or more axles, but not public passenger-carrying vehicles; towed vehicles cannot exceed 4600 kg
  • Class 4 unrestricted –All class 5 vehicles, plus an ambulance, taxi, or school bus, special activity bus with seating capacity of not more than 25 persons, including driver
  • Class 5/7 –Any 2-axle motor vehicles (other than a motorcycle), motorhomes, construction vehicles, may tow vehicles up to 4600 kg
  • Class 6 - Motorcycles, all terrain cycles or vehicle
