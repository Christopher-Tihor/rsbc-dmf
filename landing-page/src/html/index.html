RoadSafetyBC's Driver Medical Fitness Portal for medical practitioners

RoadSafetyBC is part of the Ministry of Public Safety and Solicitor General. This portal serves the medical community by:

  • Modernizing BC's driver medical fitness process by providing an electronic Driver's Medical Examination Report (DMER)
  • Simplifying the process of completing, submitting and monitoring the status of a DMER
  • Enhancing public safety and contributing to RoadSafetyBC's Vision Zero goal

Medical Professionals

Has RoadSafetyBC required your patient to have a DMER completed? Or do you want to check the status of a DMER you have submitted?

This portal is estimated to be available to medical practitioners in 2023. At that time if you are a medical practitioner, Medical Office Manager or Medical Office Assistant, you will be able to register for the RoadSafetyBC Medical Practitioner Portal in order to conduct DMERs online.

Medical Standards and Guidelines

RoadSafetyBC follows the Canadian Council of Motor Transport Administrators (CCMTA) Medical Standards with B.C. Specific Guidelines when making driver medical fitness decisions.

Please refer to these guidelines when you have questions regarding which medical conditions may have an impact on your patient's fitness to drive.

Do you have a concern about a driver?

If a patient has a medical condition that raises significant concern about their ability to safely operate a motor vehicle, please submit a Report of a Condition Affecting Fitness and Ability to Drive.

NOTE: Reporting a condition does not replace performing a medical assessment and submitting a DMER which RoadSafetyBC has required for your patient.

About RoadSafetyBC

At RoadSafetyBC we are responsible for determining driver medical fitness. We require certain individuals to complete a DMER. We use DMERs to assess if a driver's medical condition affects their ability to drive safely.
